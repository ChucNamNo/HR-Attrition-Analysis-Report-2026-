# HR Analytics Dashboard - Employee Attrition Analysis

## Introduction
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition rates using an HR Analytics dataset. The dashboard provides a comprehensive view of **1,470 employees**, diving deep into factors such as age, gender, department, education level, and job satisfaction to identify the root causes behind employee turnover.

## Objectives
From the perspective of an **HR Manager**, this report aims to answer two critical questions: 
1. **Who is leaving?**
2. **Why are they leaving?**

The ultimate goal is to identify high-risk groups, propose data-driven retention policies, and improve the workplace environment to reduce the overall **Attrition Rate**.

## Analysis
*   **Overview Metrics:** Out of **1,470 employees**, **237** have left, resulting in an **Attrition Rate of 16.12%**. The average age of the workforce is 37 years.
*   **Department:** The **R&D department** recorded the highest turnover (133 people, 56.12% of total attrition), followed by Sales (92) and HR (12).
*   **Age & Gender:** The **25-34 age group** has the highest number of departures (112). Males tend to leave more than females (150 vs 87).
*   **Education:** Employees with backgrounds in **Life Sciences** (89) and **Medical** (63) lead the attrition count.
*   **Job Satisfaction:** Key roles like *Sales Executive* and *Research Scientist* show high levels of low satisfaction (levels 1 & 2), which correlates directly with their departure rates.

---

## Insights
*   **Youth Retention Issue:** High attrition in the 25-34 and under-25 groups suggests a lack of long-term engagement, possibly due to limited career paths or non-competitive salaries.
*   **R&D Department Risk:** Despite being a core department, R&D accounts for over half of the attrition. This signals high work pressure or a gap in specialized incentive policies.
*   **Satisfaction-Attrition Link:** A significant number of staff in key roles (Laboratory Technician, Sales Executive) are dissatisfied. Without workplace improvements, the 16.12% rate is likely to rise.

---

## Dataset Limitation and Improvement
To gain deeper insights, I recommend the following data improvements:
1.  **Specific Exit Reasons:** Integrate data from Exit Interviews (Salary, Management, Work-life balance).
2.  **Compensation Data:** Include current salary vs. market benchmarks to analyze financial impact on attrition.
3.  **Tenure Details:** Track how long employees stay before leaving to identify "critical turnover years."
4.  **Consistency:** Track data quarterly or annually to identify seasonal trends (e.g., post-bonus departures).
